APE-Prediction ACC/PROD deployment — the short answer

For Asset Scheduling (Team Green) · 2026-06-26 · one-page fold of all three questions

The structure is sound; the CI flags are the risk. ACC and PROD use separate config folders, separate clusters, and separate Event Hubs, and prod only deploys on a manual ArgoCD sync. But the CI pipeline writes the new image tag into every environment folder whose flag is true in one commit — and today test-env, acc-env and prod-env are all true (group 604). So an ACC-intended build also rewrites the PROD desired-state in git. Nothing auto-runs in prod, but the prod app goes OutOfSync with the ACC change — which is the "ACC appeared in PROD" you saw. Fix before breaking changes: don't just flip the shared prod-env flag — it's a race and would freeze prod promotion. Make env selection a per-env pipeline/parameter, or treat the manual prod sync as the deliberate, audited gate.

One image (the "book"); three per-env order cards in one shared catalog (VPP-Configuration/main); a per-cluster librarian (ArgoCD) that restocks acc/prod only by hand. The CI clerk currently rewrites all three cards at once.

  1. Make env selection structural, not a shared flag — split per-env pipelines/groups or use a queue-time parameter. Avoid toggling prod-env on shared group 604 per build (race + freezes prod promotion).
