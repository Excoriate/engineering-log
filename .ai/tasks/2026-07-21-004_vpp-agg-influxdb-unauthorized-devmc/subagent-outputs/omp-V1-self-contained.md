## Top-3 must-fix

1. **Make the two-plane procedure executable.** Separate Azure and AVD/OpenShift commands and define a secure, exact credential hand-off; no variable may silently cross shells.
2. **Eliminate every placeholder and repair credential lifetimes.** Deterministically assign the Secret/key, consumer array, InfluxDB pod, admin session, new token, and old authorization ID; remove the premature `unset ADMIN` and make the password fallback rejoin the command path.
3. **Make both repair routes and the close gate pasteable.** Branch B needs a complete CSI-reconcile sequence, and §8 must prove a post-rollout write for every newly discovered consumer before old-token revocation.

## Verdict

**NOT-READY**

The stakes claim is HIGH: executing the runbook unchanged can stall after credential creation, expose or mis-handle token material during an invented cross-plane hand-off, or revoke/declare recovery without proving every consumer. A falsifying cold-run would show every command succeeding from the explicitly labelled planes with no manual placeholder substitution, the admin-password branch rejoining token creation, and a distinct post-rollout write witnessed for every discovered consumer; the current text cannot produce that run.
