## Sections that are unambiguous

- §0’s prerequisites, four hard STOP conditions, and explicit “Never” prohibitions are clear as policy. The destination for escalation is not clear; that defect is isolated above.
- §1’s fixed identifiers are concrete and directly reusable.
- The two mental-model diagrams clearly express the intended diagnostic ordering and the high-level branch outcomes. They do not repair the command-level dead branches identified above.
- §6C is unambiguous: missing org/bucket or no authentication means halt without recreating state.
- §10’s explicit prohibitions against recreating state or touching unrelated credentials are clear; only its final “confirm nothing automated” instruction is under-specified.
- The challenge-defense and self-test answers are conceptually clear, but several answers point back to execution steps that are not one-shot executable.
- No full execution section from §2 through §9 is ambiguity-free.

## Top 3 must-fix

1. **Define the two-plane secret workflow end to end.** The runbook separates `az` and AVD/`oc` but uses `$ADMIN` and the new token as if one shell owns both. Label every command’s terminal, specify an approved hidden transfer in both directions, and fix the premature `unset ADMIN`.
2. **Make token identity and creation deterministic.** Add a non-disclosing exact match from the running pod token to one authorization ID/status/scope, then capture `NEW_TOKEN`, `NEW_AUTH_ID`, and `OLD_AUTH_ID` without printing or placeholders. Complete the admin-password-success route instead of sending it to commands that still require the dead admin token.
3. **Replace the false per-deployment verification with attributable evidence.** Capture post-rollout pod/role-instance identities and timestamps, then require a positive scheduled execution and an attributed post-rollout write for each consumer. A global bucket timestamp and absence of exception rows cannot prove every rolled deployment recovered.

## Verdict

**NOT-READY**

The document has multiple blocking branches where a context-free executor must invent a shell boundary, secret-transfer mechanism, token-to-authorization identity mapping, password-recovery procedure, Branch-B sequence, dynamic rollout list, or per-consumer success witness. It is not safe for one-shot execution until those choices are encoded as commands and exhaustive decision rules.
